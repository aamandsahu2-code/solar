Electrical Definitions - Part 1 (Jan 03 Data)

\(P_{max}\)

Represents the highest wattage the panel can produce under specific test conditions.

\(I_{sc}\) (Short Circuit Current)

The max current a solar panel can produce when its + & - are directly connected (creating a Short Circuit).

\(V_{oc}\) (Voltage Open Circuit)

The max voltage a solar panel can produce when no electrical load is connected to it.

Temp & Temp IR

  • Temp IR: Infrared thermography used for temperature measurement and analysis of solar panels.
  • Temp: The operating temp of the solar panel & its impact on energy production.

Electrical Definitions - Part 2 (Jan 03 Data)

Temperature Coefficients

  • Temp K: The temperature coefficient of power.
  • Temp PT1000: A high precision temp sensor used to measure the temp of the solar panel's backsheet.

MPP Parameters

  • \(I_{mp}\): The amperage the panel produces when operating at its peak efficiency.
  • \(V_{mp}\): It is the specific voltage at which a solar panel produces its peak power output (Voltage at Max Power).

Hipot Test Details - DCW & IR (Jan 20 Data)

1. DCW (Dielectrical Voltage Withstand) Test

  • Purpose: Used for checking current leakage of a solar module.
  • Formula: \(System \text{ voltage} \times 3 + 300 \text{ voltage}\)
  • Example: \(1500 \times 3 + 300 = 4800V\)
  • Fail Condition: Busbar or Matrix shift or touch to frames.

2. IR (Insulation Resistance) Test

  • Purpose: Used to check Insulation Resistance of a Module.
  • Voltage: System voltage = \(1500V\).
  • Fail Condition: Busbar or Matrix shift or touch to the frames.

Hipot Test Details - GND & Specs (Jan 21 Data)

3. GND (Ground Continuity) Test

  • Purpose: Resistance test / Check current leakage.
  • Formula: \(JB \text{ diode current} \times 2.5\)
  • Example: \(30A \times 2.5 = 75A\).
  • Fail Condition: Frame gap / JB short.

Technical Standards (Jan 18 Data)

STC (Standard Test Conditions)

Reference conditions under which performance & rating are measured.

  • Irradiance: \(1000 W/m^2\) (Sunlight Intensity)
  • Cell temp: \(25^\circ C\)
  • Air mass: \(1.5\) spectrum

BSTC (Bifacial Standard Test Conditions)

Reference conditions for bifacial solar modules.

  • Front side Irradiance: \(1000 W/m^2\)
  • Back side Irradiance: \(135 W/m^2\)
  • Cell temp: \(25^\circ C\)
  • Air Mass: \(1.5\) spectrum

Key Formulas (Jan 19 & 22 Data)

1. Fill Factor (FF)

Ratio of max power output to the product of its \(V_{oc}\) & \(I_{sc}\).

$$FF\% = \frac{P_{max}}{V_{oc} \times I_{sc}} \times 100$$ $$FF = \frac{I_{mp} \times V_{mp}}{V_{oc} \times I_{sc}}$$

Condition: \((>75\%)\)

2. Efficiency %

$$\text{Efficiency}\% = \frac{P_{max}}{\text{Area} \times \text{Irradiance}} \times 100$$

Additional Formulas (Jan 22 Data)

3. \(I_{mp}\)

$$I_{mp} = P_{max} / V_{mp}$$

(Max power / voltage at max power)

4. \(P_{max}\)

$$P_{max} = I_{mp} \times V_{mp}$$

5. Area of a Module

Formula: \(L \times W\)

  • Example 1: \(2.278 \times 1.134 = 2.58 m^2\)
  • Example 2: \(2.382 \times 1.134 = 2.70 m^2\)
